— 01 · Manifesto

You don't call a moose.
You answer the bush.

My grandfather put a birch bark horn in my hands when I was eight. He told me: “Listen before you blow.” Thirty years later, I pass the same thing on — no shortcuts, no gimmicks.

Moose Calling 101 was born from a frustration: too many hunters step into the bush with a plastic call and no respect for the animal. My approach is different. Learning to call is learning to read the wind, the spruce, the tracks — and to hush when the forest speaks.

Every custom video, every coaching session honours knowledge that has been carried down for generations. It belongs to no one. It is only borrowed, practiced, and handed forward.

02 · Repertoire

The three voices of the bush.

A moose speaks in more than one tongue. From the bull's deep territorial grunt to the cow's plaintive drawn-out wail, every call has its season, its wind, its truth.

№ 01

The cow call

Alces americana · long wail

The drawn-out, plaintive wail. This is the king call of the rut. Done right, it pulls a bull across two kilometres of black spruce.

Season · late September ▶ Listen
№ 02

The bull grunt

Alces americana · short bellow

Short, low, territorial. A challenge between bulls, often paired with the rake of antlers on hardwood. The secret weapon of early season.

Season · early October ▶ Listen
№ 03

The calf cry

Alces americana · high-pitched bleat

The least known, the most moving. A thin cry that brings the cow back — and often the bull that follows her. Use sparingly. It is a sacred call.

Season · all year ▶ Listen
